12-WEEK MASS BUILDER
Hypertrophy Program · Beginner–Intermediate
6 days/week
3 phases · 4 weeks each
Push · Pull · Legs · Rest · Upper · Lower
Progressive Overload
🍗
Nutrition
Eat 300–500 cal surplus. Aim 1g protein per lb bodyweight daily. Don't neglect carbs — they fuel training.
💤
Sleep
7–9 hours. Muscle is built during recovery, not in the gym. This is non-negotiable.
💧
Hydration
3–4L water per day minimum. Performance drops fast when dehydrated.
📈
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5–5kg to compound lifts every 1–2 weeks when you hit the top rep range with good form.
📓
Track Everything
Log every set, rep, and weight. You cannot progress what you don't measure.
⚠️
Ego-Free Zone
Start lighter than you think. Form first, always. Injuries set you back months, not days.
Phase 1
Foundation & Form (Weeks 1–4). Higher reps (10–15), moderate weight. Focus on mind-muscle connection, mastering movement patterns, and building work capacity. Rest 60–90s between sets. Every exercise should feel controlled — no momentum, no ego.
Week 1–2 Learn the movements. Use a weight where the last 2 reps of each set are challenging but form stays perfect.
Week 3–4 Add 2.5–5kg to compounds where form allows. Cables and machines: increase by 1 plate step.
Monday — Push
Chest · Shoulders · Triceps
Warm-Up (10–12 min)
  • 5 min light cardio (treadmill walk or bike) — raise heart rate
  • Arm circles: 15 reps forward + backward
  • Band pull-aparts or face pulls with light band: 2 × 20
  • Push-up with pause at bottom: 2 × 10 (activates chest & shoulders)
  • Rotator cuff external rotation with light band: 2 × 15 each arm
  • Warm-up sets: For Bench Press — do 1×10 empty bar, 1×8 at 50% working weight, 1×4 at 75% working weight before your first working set
Tuesday — Pull
Back · Biceps · Rear Delts
Warm-Up (10–12 min)
  • 5 min light cardio or rowing machine
  • Cat-cow stretches: 10 reps (mobilises thoracic spine)
  • Dead hang from pull-up bar: 30 seconds × 2 (decompresses spine, activates lats)
  • Scapular pull-ups: 2 × 10 (no elbow bend — pure scapular depression)
  • Light cable row: 2 × 15 (50% working weight — groove the movement)
  • Warm-up sets: Lat Pulldown — 1×12 at 50% working weight, 1×8 at 70%
Wednesday — Legs
Quads · Hamstrings · Glutes · Calves
Warm-Up (12–15 min)
  • 5–7 min bike or treadmill incline walk
  • Bodyweight squats: 2 × 15 (feel the glutes and quads activating)
  • Hip circles: 10 each direction (hip flexor mobility)
  • Glute bridges on floor: 2 × 20 (activate posterior chain before loading)
  • Leg swings front/back: 15 each leg
  • Walking lunges: 2 × 10 each leg (dynamic warm-up)
  • Warm-up sets: Hack Squat — 1×10 empty sled, 1×8 at 50% working weight
Thursday — Rest / Light Jog
Active Recovery
🔄
Recovery Day
This is where you actually grow. Your muscles repair during rest, not during lifting.

Option A — Complete Rest: Light walking, stretching, foam rolling.
Option B — Light Jog: 20–30 min easy-pace jog (65–70% max HR). Not a sprint session — you should be able to hold a conversation.

Recommended: 10–15 min foam rolling session. Focus on quads, hamstrings, lats, and thoracic spine. This dramatically improves next-day performance.
Friday — Upper Body
Chest · Back · Shoulders · Arms
Warm-Up (10–12 min)
  • 5 min bike or elliptical
  • Thoracic extension over foam roller: 60 seconds
  • Band pull-aparts: 2 × 20
  • Face pulls light cable: 2 × 15
  • Incline push-ups: 2 × 10
  • Warm-up sets: Incline Barbell — 1×10 empty bar, 1×6 at 60% working weight
Saturday — Lower Body
Glutes · Hamstrings · Quads · Calves
Warm-Up (12–15 min)
  • 5–7 min rowing machine or bike
  • 90/90 hip stretch: 60 sec each side
  • Banded clamshells: 2 × 20 each side (glute medius activation)
  • Single-leg glute bridges: 2 × 12 each leg
  • Bodyweight Romanian deadlift: 2 × 12 (groove hip hinge pattern)
  • Warm-up sets: Hip Thrust — 2×10 bodyweight, 1×8 with empty bar before loading
